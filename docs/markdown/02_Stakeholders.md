# 1. Introduction

Before developing any software system, it is important to identify the people, groups, and systems that are connected to the project.

These people or groups are called **stakeholders**.

A stakeholder is anyone who:

* uses the system,
* manages the system,
* develops the system,
* benefits from the system,
* or is affected by the system.

Understanding stakeholders helps developers:

* understand requirements clearly,
* reduce misunderstandings,
* build useful features,
* improve customer satisfaction,
* and reduce project risks.

---

# 2. Definition of Stakeholders

A stakeholder is:

> "Any individual, group, or organization that can affect or be affected by the achievement of a project's objectives."

Stakeholders can be divided into two main categories:

## 1. Internal Stakeholders

People directly involved in building and operating the system.

Examples:

* Developers
* Administrators
* Business Owners
* System Analysts

---

## 2. External Stakeholders

People outside the development team who use or are affected by the system.

Examples:

* Customers
* Suppliers
* Delivery Companies
* Payment Providers

---

# 3. Importance of Stakeholder Analysis

Stakeholder analysis helps to:

### Understand user needs.

### Identify project risks.

### Improve communication.

### Prioritize system requirements.

### Increase project success rate.

Without proper stakeholder analysis, developers may build features that users do not need.
